## Writing Rules

### Tone and Voice
- Professional and compassionate. The reader is likely going through a difficult time. Speak with authority but also with empathy.
- Write in a way that everyday people can understand. Avoid unnecessary legal jargon. When legal terms must be used, briefly explain them.

### Strict Formatting Rules
- **No contractions.** Write "do not" instead of "don't", "cannot" instead of "can't", "will not" instead of "won't", etc. This applies to all content without exception.
- **No em dashes.** Do not use the "—" character. Use commas, semicolons, colons, or separate sentences instead.
- **No cliche lines.** Avoid overused phrases like "navigating the legal system", "in the eyes of the law", "when it comes to", "at the end of the day", "in today's world", etc. Write with original, direct language.
- **No guarantees of outcomes.** Never promise or imply that hiring Ikerd Law Firm will result in a specific legal outcome (dismissal, reduced sentence, settlement amount, etc.).

### Disclaimer
Every piece of content must include a disclaimer. Use this standard disclaimer or a contextually appropriate variation:

> **Disclaimer:** This content is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Every case is unique, and outcomes depend on individual circumstances. Contact Ikerd Law Firm for a consultation to discuss your specific situation.

### Citations and Links
- **Never link to competitor law firm websites.** Do not use competitor sites as citation sources.
- Use authoritative sources for citations: Louisiana state legislature (legis.la.gov), Louisiana Supreme Court, government agencies, legal databases, and reputable news outlets.
- Internal links should point to relevant pages on https://www.ikerdlaw.com when referencing related practice areas or services.

## Research and Accuracy

**THIS IS CRITICAL.** All content must be verified for accuracy before delivery.

- Always verify that statutes, laws, and legal codes cited are current and have not been amended or repealed.
- Verify all dates, case references, penalties, and sentencing ranges against current Louisiana law.
- When referencing specific statutes (e.g., La. R.S. 14:98 for DWI), confirm the statute number and its current text.
- If information cannot be verified with confidence, flag it clearly for human review rather than publishing uncertain content.
- The client and the content team can face legal liability for inaccurate legal information published on the website.

## SEO and Search Optimization

### Location Targeting
- Primary location target: **Lafayette, Louisiana**
- Include Lafayette and surrounding Acadiana region references naturally in content where appropriate.
- Use location-specific context (Lafayette Parish, Acadiana, specific local courts) to strengthen local relevance.

### Keyword Strategy
- Target keywords should be determined by the specific topic of each piece of content.
- Include primary and secondary keywords naturally. Do not keyword-stuff.
- Use semantic variations and related terms throughout the content.

### AI Overview and SERP Optimization
All content must be structured to maximize visibility in search results:

- **AI Overview optimization:** Write clear, direct answers to common questions early in the content. Use concise paragraph summaries that AI can extract.
- **Schema markup:** Include appropriate JSON-LD structured data with every piece of content (Article, FAQPage, LegalService, Attorney, LocalBusiness as appropriate).
- **Alt text:** Provide descriptive, keyword-relevant alt text for all images.
- **Internal linking:** Every piece of content must include internal links to related practice area pages and service pages on ikerdlaw.com.
- **Header hierarchy:** Use proper H1, H2, H3 structure with keyword-rich headers.
- **Featured snippet targeting:** Format key information as numbered lists, bullet points, or concise definitions to increase snippet eligibility.
- **Meta description:** Provide a compelling meta description (150-160 characters) with the primary keyword and a call to action.

## Content Types

When creating content, follow these type-specific guidelines:

### Blog Posts
- 800-1500 words unless otherwise specified
- Include a compelling title with the primary keyword
- Open with a hook that addresses the reader's concern directly
- Include at least 3 internal links to related ikerdlaw.com pages
- End with a clear call to action to contact Ikerd Law Firm

### Website / Service Pages
- Focus on one practice area per page
- Lead with what the reader needs to know about their situation
- Include Louisiana-specific legal information (statutes, penalties, processes)
- Include a call to action with contact information

### FAQs
- Write questions in the exact language a potential client would use
- Keep answers concise but thorough
- Use FAQ schema markup for every Q&A pair

### Social Media
- Adapt tone to be slightly more conversational while maintaining professionalism
- Still no contractions, no em dashes, no cliches
- Include a call to action where appropriate
